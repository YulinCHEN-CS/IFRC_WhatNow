Testing Strategy

To make sure that all the features implemented are working properly, our team tested both the front-end and the back-end. During the development process, we also invited our customers and members of other groups to conduct UAT tests to simulate the most realistic scenarios

Unit and integration testing

User acceptance testing

To conduct User Acceptance Testing (UAT), our team carefully selected three individuals from our network of classmates, clients, and friends to simulate the roles of an API user, NS_admin (National Society Administrator), NS_editor (National Society Editor), and gdpc_admin. We created a detailed to-do list to guide them through a series of predefined tasks and operations. After completing the test, we asked each participant to rate their experience and provide valuable feedback and suggestions based on their firsthand interactions with the product. This process not only helped us gain insights into the practical application of our product from the user's perspective but also highlighted specific areas for improvement, enabling us to better meet the needs and expectations of our users.

Tester1 Feedback

The simple UI makes it convenient to use even in case of emergency. However, there are several drawbacks: When the page slides down, the font size in the navigation bar does not change, while the rest of the navigation bar becomes smaller and the proportions are out of balance. The font color in the sub-navigation bar is too light and does not contrast enough with the background color.

For the feature part, There is no problem with the overall functionality. However, the guidance system of the page is not very perfect, and if you are a new user, it is easy to not know what to do next.

Tester2 Feedback

The UI design is good overall, using a simple design to make the page not dazzling, and there is no distraction from the information to disrupt the use. However, there is still some room for improvement. The animations are not smooth enough, the PROFILE and LOGOUT buttons are too large and the word in the middle is a bit small. Every time you click on the filter box, it will cause the navigation bar to move slightly, which can be uncomfortable.

The feature part is generally fine, but whenever I finish modifying the hazard content and go back to the previous page, the original filter of the page will ask to be re-selected, which is inconvenient.

Tester3 Feedback

The UI design is good and overall comfortable to use. But there is a small flaw, which is that when the cursor moves to WhatNow message in the navigation, the red underline below is not continuous. In addition, the footer part of the website is relatively rudimentary.

For Feature part, Overall, it was no problem and there were almost no bugs. But in the edit content section: when I add a language, the page always goes to its original form when it is finished, and I need to re-select society, language, and region, which is inconvenient.

Conclution

Based on the feedback from the three testers, it's clear that while the User Interface (UI) design is generally appreciated for its simplicity and ease of use, especially in emergency situations, there are notable areas that require improvement to enhance the overall user experience. Key issues include inconsistent sizing and color contrast in the navigation bar, suboptimal guidance for new users, unsmooth animations, and inconvenient behavior of certain interactive elements like the filter box and navigation adjustments.

The functionality and features of the platform, while generally meeting the users' needs, also present challenges in terms of usability. Users noted inconveniences such as the need to re-select filters or settings after completing tasks, which can disrupt the workflow and decrease efficiency.

To conclude, the feedback underscores the importance of not only maintaining a clean and simple UI design but also ensuring that visual elements are consistently and intuitively designed. Moreover, improving the guidance system for new users, refining animations, and fixing issues with interactive elements could significantly improve user satisfaction. Addressing these feedback points will not only make the platform more user-friendly but also enhance the overall effectiveness of the platform in achieving its intended purpose. Future iterations of the platform should focus on these areas to provide a seamless and efficient experience for all users.
