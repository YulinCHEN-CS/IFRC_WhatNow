Abstract

"WhatNow" is an innovative web-based application developed against the backdrop of the increasing frequency and intensity of natural disasters and emergencies due to climate change and environmental degradation. The service aims to provide subscribers with timely and accurate information about nearby disasters, highlighting the growing importance of access to disaster information and preventive measures.

The core functionality of WhatNow is to alert users about potential impending disasters and provide corresponding strategies to deal with these events. This service targets a diverse user base, including individuals, communities, and government agencies. For individuals, it offers a means to protect themselves and their families by taking precautionary measures before disasters strike. Communities and government agencies benefit from it as an effective tool for planning and implementing disaster response and mitigation measures, thereby reducing potential losses and impacts.

The inception of WhatNow is rooted in the necessity for accessible safety information and the utilization of modern technology to enhance disaster response capabilities. By offering real-time information, an early warning system, and suggested solutions, WhatNow provides critical support in disaster management and emergency response, helping to mitigate the impact of disasters on human society.
